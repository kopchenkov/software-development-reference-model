# Software Development Reference Model — Capabilities Guide

This document contains the complete list of all 58 capabilities (Capabilities) included in the reference model for a software development company.

---

## DEVELOPMENT TEAM CAPABILITIES

### Block 1: Product Management

**Product Management** — Managing the product lifecycle from idea to market launch, including defining vision, development strategy, prioritization, and coordination with stakeholders to maximize business and user value.

**Backlog Management** — Maintaining and prioritizing a single ranked list of requirements, user stories, tasks, and defects, ensuring transparency of development plans and readiness of items for implementation in upcoming sprints.

**Release Planning Management** — Defining timelines, scope, and content of releases, coordinating dependencies, managing delivery risks, and synchronizing with marketing and commercial activities.

**Product Analytics Management** — Collecting, processing, and interpreting data on user behavior and product usage to make informed product decisions, validate hypotheses, and assess the effectiveness of implemented changes.

**User Experience (UX/UI) Management** — Designing interfaces and interactions that ensure intuitiveness, accessibility, and user satisfaction, including user research, prototyping, and testing design solutions.

**Requirements Management** — Eliciting, analyzing, documenting, validating, and tracking product requirements throughout the lifecycle, ensuring they are unambiguous, complete, and aligned with business goals.

---

### Block 2: Development & Delivery

**Development & Delivery** — A comprehensive capability that unites all engineering practices for creating, testing, integrating, and delivering software, ensuring predictable and high-quality product releases.

**Solution Architecture Management** — Designing the technical architecture of a product considering non-functional requirements (scalability, performance, security, maintainability), selecting the technology stack, and ensuring compliance with architectural standards.

**Development (Coding)** — Writing clean, maintainable, and efficient code using agreed-upon programming languages, frameworks, and tools, including code review and refactoring.

**Version Control Management** — Controlling source code changes using version control systems, managing branching, resolving merge conflicts, and ensuring the integrity of the development history.

**CI/CD (Continuous Integration & Delivery)** — Automating build, testing, and deployment of software, enabling fast and reliable promotion of changes from development to production environments.

**Testing Management** — Planning, designing, executing, and automating all types of testing (unit, integration, functional, regression, performance, acceptance) to ensure quality and prevent defects.

**Configuration Management** — Managing environment settings, application parameters, and configuration files, ensuring consistency across development, testing, and production environments.

**Monitoring & Observability** — Collecting, aggregating, and visualizing metrics, logs, and traces of application and infrastructure performance, enabling understanding of system state, anomaly detection, and problem diagnosis.

---

### Block 3: Operations & Support

**Operations & Support** — Ensuring uninterrupted operation of production systems, rapid incident response, and meeting user needs throughout all stages of operation.

**Incident Management** — Detecting, logging, classifying, investigating, and resolving incidents affecting service availability or performance, minimizing recovery time.

**Problem Management** — Identifying and eliminating root causes of recurring incidents, managing known errors, and implementing preventive measures to prevent future failures.

**Change Management** — Standardized management of all changes to production environments, including risk assessment, approval, planning, and controlled implementation.

**Release Management** — Coordinating and executing deployment of new software versions to production environments, including preparing release artifacts, communicating with users, and rollback when necessary.

**User Support** — Providing assistance to product users on usage issues, resolving requests and problem reports, collecting feedback, and escalating defects to the development team.

---

### Block 4: Quality & Security Management

**Quality & Security Management** — Comprehensive assurance of product compliance with quality, security, and regulatory standards throughout the development lifecycle.

**Code Quality Management** — Assessing and controlling source code quality through static analysis, automated checks of style, complexity, test coverage, and other metrics that ensure maintainability and reliability.

**Information Security Management** — Protecting information, applications, and infrastructure from threats, including vulnerability management, attack prevention, data encryption, and security monitoring.

**Access Management** — Managing identity, authentication, and authorization of users and services, enforcing the principle of least privilege and controlling access to resources.

**Audit & Compliance** — Verifying that development processes and products comply with internal policies, industry standards, and regulatory requirements, conducting internal audits, and preparing for external inspections.

---

### Block 5: Team Leadership & Administration

**Team Leadership & Administration** — Ensuring effective operation of the development team through managing people, projects, budget, communications, and knowledge.

**Project Management** — Planning, executing, and controlling development projects, including managing timelines, resources, risks, and dependencies, ensuring achievement of project goals.

**Team Management** — Leading the development team, including task assignment, workload distribution, motivation, conducting 1-on-1 meetings, performance evaluation, and conflict resolution.

**Budget Management** — Planning, allocating, and controlling financial resources allocated to product development and operations, including budgeting for equipment, licenses, cloud resources, and external services.

**Communication Management** — Organizing effective information exchange within the team, between teams, and with external stakeholders, including stand-ups, retrospectives, demos, and expectation management.

**Knowledge Management** — Collecting, systematizing, storing, and disseminating knowledge and experience accumulated during development, including documentation, architectural decisions, design decisions, and lessons learned.

---

## CORPORATE CAPABILITIES

### Block 6: Strategy & Business Development

**Strategy & Business Development** — Defining the company's long-term goals, directions for product line development, and ways to achieve sustainable competitive advantage in target markets.

**Product Portfolio Management** — Managing the company's product portfolio as an investment portfolio: performance analysis, resource allocation, decisions on launching, developing, or retiring products.

**Go-to-Market Management** — Planning and executing a comprehensive set of activities to launch new products or major updates to the market, including positioning, pricing, communication campaigns, and training.

**Vendor Relationship Management** — Selecting, evaluating, and managing strategic partners, technology suppliers, contractors, and integrators, including negotiations and monitoring of obligations fulfillment.

**Intellectual Property Management** — Identifying, registering, protecting, and commercializing intellectual property assets (patents, copyrights, trademarks, know-how) and managing licenses for third-party code usage.

---

### Block 7: Sales, Marketing & Customer Relations

**Sales, Marketing & Customer Relations** — A comprehensive capability for acquiring, retaining, and growing the customer base, covering all stages of the sales funnel and post-sales service.

**Sales Management** — Managing the sales process from lead qualification to deal closure, including funnel management, revenue forecasting, preparing commercial proposals, and negotiations.

**Marketing Management** — Planning and implementing marketing strategies and campaigns, including branding, demand generation, content marketing, digital marketing, and market trend analysis.

**Customer Relationship Management (CRM)** — Building and maintaining long-term relationships with customers, managing feedback, loyalty programs, regular strategy meetings, and requirement gathering.

**Contract & SLA Management** — Developing, negotiating, concluding, executing, and monitoring contracts with customers and partners, including managing service levels, payment terms, and liability.

---

### Block 8: Corporate Governance & Administration

**Corporate Governance & Administration** — Establishing and maintaining the company's governance system, ensuring effective decision-making, legal compliance, human capital development, and financial sustainability.

**Organizational Structure Management** — Designing and evolving the company's organizational structure, allocating areas of responsibility, building reporting lines, and ensuring effective coordination between departments.

**Financial Management** — Managing the company's financial resources, including budgeting, cash flow planning, profitability management, management and accounting reporting, and cost control.

**Legal Risk Management** — Identifying, assessing, and mitigating legal risks of the company's activities, legal support for transactions, contract work, litigation representation, and corporate law compliance.

**Corporate Culture & Talent Management** — Shaping and maintaining corporate culture and values, attracting, retaining, and developing talented employees, building career paths, motivation systems, and performance evaluation.

**Regulatory Reporting Management** — Preparing and submitting mandatory reports (tax, accounting, statistical) in accordance with requirements of government agencies and regulators, ensuring compliance with all established deadlines and formats.

**Internal Reporting Management** — Generating internal management reports (dashboards, KPI analytics) for leadership on operational, financial, HR, and other key performance indicators of the company.

---

### Block 9: Infrastructure & Corporate Services

**Infrastructure & Corporate Services** — Providing the company with the necessary internal IT infrastructure, facilities, and administrative services for efficient operation of all departments.

**Internal IT Infrastructure Management** — Maintaining and evolving the company's internal IT environment: networks, servers, workstations, corporate applications (email, messengers, office suite), employee access management, and user technical support.

**Facilities & Administrative Management** — Managing office premises, facilities services, access control, supplies of consumables, event organization, and other administrative services.

**Corporate Data Management** — Managing data as a corporate asset: developing data governance policies, data quality management, metadata management, data architecture, and ensuring integrity and security.

**Enterprise Architecture Management** — Coherent design and integration of all internal IT systems and services of the company (ERP, CRM, HRM, development management systems, monitoring, etc.) into a unified, consistent architecture.

**Procurement Management** — Organizing the company's procurement activities: identifying needs, selecting suppliers, conducting tenders, contracting for licenses, cloud resources, equipment, and services, inventory management.

---

### Block 10: Business Continuity & Risk Management

**Business Continuity & Risk Management** — Protecting the company from adverse events by identifying risks, developing response plans, and ensuring the ability of the business to continue operating during crisis situations.

**Business Continuity Management** — Developing and maintaining plans to ensure continuity of key business processes in the event of serious incidents (outages, cyberattacks, loss of key employees), managing post-failure recovery.

**Enterprise Risk Management** — Systematic identification, assessment, monitoring, and management of all categories of risks affecting the achievement of the company's strategic goals (financial, operational, reputational, regulatory).
